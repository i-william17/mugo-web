Project Background

The idea for the smart walking stick project originated from Mr. Jasin telling me to search about how AI/robots are helping the visually impaired.
When you take a look at how the ordinary stick works, you can tell that it if not efficient since the obstacle has to be tapped to be felt. What if the obstacle is a person. What if there is no obstacle. What if there was an easy way to help the visually impiared navigate. That is where my project comes in. A lot of research has been conducted to ensure a seamless transition to a smart walking stick

Integrated GPS System

The smart walking stick includes a built-in GPS which can track down the person using the stick.

Emergency Alert Button

In case of an emergency, users can press the alert button to alert their watchers from home to come to their aid as soon as possible.

Bluetooth Connectivity

Connect the walking stick to your smartphone via Bluetooth because you can control using your phone (only some things).

Adjustable height and grip

The walking stick should be adjustable to accommodate individual user heights and feature a comfortable, ergonomic grip for extended use.

Lightweight and Durable

Consideration for lightweight materials yet sturdy is important for ease of use and durability.

Long battery life

The walking stick should have a long-lasting battery to ensure that the user can rely on it throughout the day without frequent recharging.

Auditory and Tactile Feedback

Incorporating features such as vibration or auditory feedback for different situations, such as approaching stairs or doors, can greatly enhance the user experience.
